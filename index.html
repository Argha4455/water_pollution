Save water Save life
  1. Defination:
    • Water Pollution refers to the contamination of water bodies, including lakes, rivers, oceans, aquifers, reservoirs, and groundwater. It occurs due to the release of substances into these water sources, interfering with their beneficial use and disrupting natural ecosystems.
  2. Types and Sources of Water Pollution
    • Pathogenic Microorganism:Harmful bacteria, viruses, and parasites.
    • Putrescible Organic Waste:Decomposing organic matter.
    • Toxic Chemicals:Industrial discharges and chemical spills.
    • Sediments:Soil erosion and construction activities.
  3. Impact of Water Pollution:
    • Human Health:Contaminated water can cause diseases and affect drinking water quality.
    • Aquatic Wildlife:Pollutants harm fish, amphibians, and other aquatic organisms.
    • Ecosystems:Disruption of natural processes and loss of biodiversity.
  4. Solution and Migration:
    • Waste Treatment:Proper treatment of swage and industrial effluents.
    • Regultaion:Stricter laws and monitoring of pollution sources.
    • Public Awareness: people about responsible waste disposal.
